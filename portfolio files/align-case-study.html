Reflections & Learnings

What I Learned About Technical Product Management

This project taught me that the best product solutions aren't always the most feature-rich‚Äîthey're the ones that solve the right problem at the root level. Initially, I considered building additional validation layers on top of the existing API. But by investing time in understanding the actual translation logic failures through data analysis, I realized we needed to redesign the core mapping system itself.


Key insight: In B2B2C products, every system failure has a ripple effect. A delayed order isn't just a technical issue‚Äîit's a doctor losing trust in the platform and a patient experiencing delayed care. This experience reinforced that PM success in technical products requires balancing system architecture decisions with deep empathy for end-user impact.


What I'd do differently: I would have involved the operations team even earlier in the requirements definition phase. Their hands-on experience with manual interventions provided invaluable context that could have accelerated our problem scoping. In future projects, I'll prioritize including operational stakeholders from day one, not just during implementation.
